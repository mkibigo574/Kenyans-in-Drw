About Us

A Home Away From Home in the Northern Territory

The Kenyans In Darwin (KIDA) is a not-for-profit association bringing together Kenyans living, working and studying across Darwin and the wider Top End.

From quiet bush dinners to lively cultural celebrations, KIDA exists to keep our Kenyan heritage alive while building strong, supportive relationships that make life in Australia feel a little closer to home.

Whether you're newly arrived, raising a family, or have been part of the Territory's story for years — you belong here.

Welfare Program

Standing Together in Times of Loss

KIDA members can rely on a dedicated welfare fund that provides financial assistance and solidarity during bereavement in the nuclear family.

How the Welfare Fund Works

Every active KIDA member contributes to a shared welfare pool. When a member loses someone in their nuclear family, the fund releases an immediate contribution to help with funeral costs, repatriation, and family support — whether the loss is here in Australia or back home in Kenya.

The welfare team coordinates with the bereaved family to ensure dignity, privacy and care every step of the way.

Nuclear Family Coverage Includes

  • The member themselves
  • Legally recognised spouse
  • Biological and legally adopted children
  • Biological parents of the member
  • Parents-in-law (spouse's parents)
